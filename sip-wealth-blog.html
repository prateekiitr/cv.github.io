01 — The SIP Illusion

That ₹1 Crore Is
Lying To You

Picture this: You're 25. You open a finance YouTube video. A cheerful calculator shows that ₹2,000 a month — less than a Netflix subscription — will make you a crorepati by 52. You almost feel smug. But wait…

Here is the pitch you've seen a hundred times: Start a ₹2,000/month SIP at age 25, step it up 10% every year, and by 52 you'll have ₹1 Crore. The math checks out. The emotion doesn't. Because that ₹1 Crore isn't arriving in 1997 — it's arriving in 2052.

With India running at ~6% inflation, your future ₹1 Crore has the purchasing power of roughly ₹21 Lakhs today. That is not a retirement plan.

₹1 Crore in 27 years is ₹21 Lakhs in today's money. If this is your entire financial plan, please read on.

The fix isn't to stop SIPing. Use the calculator. Move the sliders. Set a corpus target that means something in future money — not a number that feels big but buys very little.

Start at ₹2,000. Build the habit. Then add a zero every time life lets you.

"Start small." But always know what that number looks like after inflation eats it.

You are better than you give yourself credit for. Add that zero when life lets you. And it will.

02 — The Passive Income Trap

You Can't Buy Freedom
on Dividend Yield

You've seen the Instagram reel. Someone holding a coffee in Bali, caption reading "my dividends paid for this." You screenshot it. You search "top dividend stocks India." Here's what the reel doesn't show you.

Let's actually run the numbers. Say your expenses are ₹40,000/month. At 3% dividend yield you need ₹1.6 Crores invested — right now. And here's the brutal twist: if you had ₹1.6 Crores, dividend stocks would be one of the worst places to put it.

Another problem with dividend stocks: A company paying out earnings has fewer rupees to reinvest in growth. Fast-growing companies don't pay dividends — they have too many good ideas for where to put that money. If the stock halves, your 3% yield becomes meaningless.

Equity exists for capital appreciation over the long term. Not monthly income.

The single best passive income strategy in your 20s costs nothing: get a better salary. Every ₹10,000 added to income compounds into crores over a lifetime.
03 — The Diderot Effect

How a Salary Raise
Makes You Poorer

Denis Diderot was gifted an expensive scarlet robe. Beautiful — but now his wooden desk looked shabby next to it. New desk. New chair. New drapes. He ended up in debt. One upgrade triggered a spiral. Sound familiar?

You get a ₹20,000 raise. New apartment. Furniture looks cheap. New sofa. New phone. Suddenly you're spending ₹30,000 more than before the raise — and your investments haven't moved. This is lifestyle inflation, and it is the silent wealth-killer nobody talks about.

If the score exceeds 1.0, your lifestyle has become a cage — you can no longer come down without feeling like you've failed. The purpose of money is freedom, not a bigger CTC on LinkedIn.

Increase your SIP before you increase your apartment. The SIP grows. The apartment just needs renovation in 5 years.

Every time you get a raise, increase your SIP first. Then decide what to do with the rest. In that order. Always.
04 — Always Last

Why You Always Buy
at the Exact Top

When the author moved to Singapore as a kid, everyone had been playing badminton for 5 years. He was terrible — not because he was bad, but because he was late. Every hot asset class works exactly the same way.

Right now gold is at all-time highs. You're thinking about gold. Before that, AI stocks. Before that, crypto. By the time you move your money — the people who made money are already thinking about their exit. You are not early. You are the exit liquidity.

The Playbook

What Actually Works
In Your 20s

None of this is complicated. The people who get wealthy in their 40s simply didn't make these four obvious mistakes in their 20s — when it still mattered most.

Step 01
SIP — but with inflation in your head
₹2,000 starts the habit. But your real corpus target must be inflation-adjusted. If you need ₹5Cr in today's money, your nominal target is ₹12–15Cr. Plan for that — not the comfortable lie.
Step 02
Grow your active income — obsessively
The biggest lever in your 20s isn't your CAGR — it's your salary. Every ₹10,000 added monthly, invested over 20 years, is worth crores. Negotiate. Upskill. Switch jobs.
Step 03
Raise your SIP before your lifestyle
Every raise: SIP first, lifestyle second. Keep your lock-in score below 1.0. A lifestyle you can't come down from is a lifestyle that owns you.
Step 04
Own all asset classes — always
Equity, gold, debt, eventually real estate. When something trends on Twitter, you already own it. You don't chase trends. You rebalance toward them.

Money buys freedom. Not a bigger CTC.
Not a lifestyle you can't exit.

Start small. Think in real terms. Grow income relentlessly. Stay diversified. That's the entire playbook — boring is what actually works.
