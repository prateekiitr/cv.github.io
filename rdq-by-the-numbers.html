RDQ, by the Numbers

No narrative this time — just every stat from the paper that actually mattered, in one scroll. If you only have two minutes, this is the post.

I've written the diagnosis post and the results post already. This one skips the story and just lays out the numbers, in the order I'd want them if someone handed me this paper cold.

The Diagnosis
r = 0.999
Correlation between total KL drift and log-perplexity
Across every method and bit-width tested. p < 0.001. This is the single number the whole paper hangs on.
6,548×
Growth in residual stream variance across depth
σ² ≈ 10⁻⁴ at early layers, ≈ 0.65 by layer 32. Later layers are categorically more fragile to upstream drift.
27 / 32
Layers that fail Kolmogorov–Smirnov normality
84% non-Gaussian. Only layers {1, 7, 20, 26, 28, 30} pass. Invalidates the Gaussian assumption in most PTQ theory, including my own Theorem 2.
2.42×
RTN's perplexity ratio, W4 → W3, on LLaMA-3-8B
5.83 → 14.09 PPL, for only 1.33× more compression. The cliff the whole paper is trying to explain.
The Results
6 / 6
Model × bit-width cells where RDQ+CEC beats LeanQuant
LLaMA-3-8B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Mistral-7B — each at W3A16 and W4A16. Best published baseline, beaten every time.
−46.4%
PPL reduction vs RTN, W3A16, LLaMA-3-8B
14.09 → 7.55. The largest single gain in the paper, on the model with the most severe drift.
1–4%
Distance from FP16 at W4A16, across all three models
5.62 vs 5.557 (LLaMA), 6.38 vs 6.158 (Qwen), 5.73 vs 5.632 (Mistral). Near-lossless 4-bit.
0×
Inference overhead
Scales fold into RMSNorm weights — mathematically exact. Standard group-128 output, deployable on AIMET, GGUF, any existing stack.

The Ablation
−2.03
PPL gap from drift-aware calibration alone, Qwen W3
Same AWQ scaling formula, only the calibration data changes: 9.49 → 7.46. The whole gain, isolated.
+2.11
PPL gap where AWQ underperforms plain RTN, Qwen W4
8.50 (AWQ) vs 6.46 (RTN, no calibration at all). AWQ's salient-channel heuristic mis-transfers off LLaMA statistics.
10.31
PPL when sequential calibration runs with no AWQ scaling
Worse than RTN's 8.20. Confirms neither component works alone — you need drift-aware inputs and the scaling search together.
Reading the ablation in one line: the AWQ formula was never the problem — the calibration data was. Fix the data, the same formula goes from underperformer to SOTA.
The Cost
30 min calibration — LLaMA-3-8B, A100-80GB, W3 ~20 wall-clock hrs — 70B, unbatched ~5 wall-clock hrs — 70B, batched One-time offline cost Amortizes in <1 min of inference at 1K queries/day
Where the Theory Falls Short
1.36×
Actual PPL degradation at W3, vs. 1.06× predicted
The Gaussian-regime perplexity bound (Theorem 2) is violated, not conservative — expected, given 84% of layers are non-Gaussian. Kept as a proposition, not a theorem.
